Q&A SESSION ABOUT AUTHOR C. L. PALACIOS

In this article ❧ I created this interview to explain what is Diary of Z Girl. Hope you enjoy it!

INTERVIEWER: Are you married? C.L. PALACIOS: Happily. I've been married to Carmen for 12 years, and we have a preschooler, a naughty boy.

INT: How did you learn to make books?

CL: For 20 years, I was an academic. I was a full-time professor and published one book about the political uses of theater in the 20th century. The book was based on my PhD dissertation. Aside from that, I published a bunch of scientific papers, most of them about cultural history.

INT: So, you were a historian who fell in love with literature?

CL: Yes. During my academic career, I learned how beautiful writing was. I began to read literature a lot because I realized it made me a better writer.

INT: Are you a Swiftie?

CL: [laughs] I had to convert to that because my wife couldn't stop listening to her songs. Eventually, I accepted my destiny and even began to play some of Taylor's songs on my guitar. My wife is a terrible singer, so I had to learn the lyrics, too. My favorite song is Delicate.

INT: Where are you from?

CL: My mind is constantly in New York. I believe that reality is a creation of the mind, so you could say I'm from NYC.

INT: You've been moving from the academy to the bookstagram world. How does being a fiction writer differ from being a scholar?

CL: The staples of good writing in academia and fiction are the same: write something that blows their minds. Unfortunately, many academics don't follow that rule today. That's one of the reasons I moved from there: I want to serve people with my writing.
