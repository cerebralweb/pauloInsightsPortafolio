MULTIPLY FIRST, THEN SPLIT THE MARKETING BUDGET ACROSS EVERY OFFICE

In this article ❧ Marketing budgets are rarely built one line at a time. Teams often set one channel as a fixed multiple of another, then push the combined pool out to regions that each carry an equal share. This problem shows how a short chain of multiplication and division turns a budgeting rule into a clear figure that any office can plan against.

The Problem

A finance lead allocates 1.24 million dollars to search advertising and three times that amount to social advertising for a quarterly campaign. The combined advertising budget is then divided equally among 5 regional offices.

How many million dollars of the social advertising budget go to each office?

  • a) 37.2
  • b) 3.72
  • c) 0.744
  • d) 3/4
Click to Reveal the Answer

Correct Answer: c) 0.744 ✅

Math Solution

Find the social line by taking the search spend and multiplying it by three, which gives 3.72 million. Then divide that total by the five offices to reach the share each one receives.

An even split hands each office the same fraction of every line, so the social line can be divided on its own without rebuilding the whole budget.

Step-by-Step Breakdown

The following sequence shows how the final amount is derived:

  1. Social advertising total: 3 × 1.24 = 3.72 million dollars
  2. Combined budget: 1.24 + 3.72 = 4.96 million dollars
  3. Even split rule: each office receives one fifth of every line
  4. Social per office: 3.72 ÷ 5 = 0.744 million dollars
  5. Conclusion: each office receives 0.744 million dollars of social advertising, which is option c

Historical Background

Splitting a shared pool into equal parts is one of the oldest tasks in commercial arithmetic. In the trading cities of late medieval Italy, abbaco schools trained young merchants to scale one quantity against another and to divide profits across partners, the same two moves at work here. Those everyday calculations, taught for ledgers and partnerships, are the ancestors of the budget splits that finance teams run today.

# Scale and Split a Budget Line

This skill takes a base spend, a multiple that sets a second spend against the base, and a count of equal units. It scales the base spend by the multiple to get the second line total, divides that total evenly across the units, and reports how much of the second line each unit receives. It asks for each number as it goes and prints a plain answer that names the scenario.

---

## Dependencies

No external libraries are needed. The work is one multiplication and one division using everyday arithmetic.

---

## Inputs

- **Base spend.** The amount that goes to the first line, entered as a decimal in millions of dollars.
- **Multiple.** How many times larger the second line is than the base line, entered as a decimal.
- **Number of units.** The count of equal units such as offices or regions, entered as a whole number.

---

## Steps

### Step 1. State the goal

Open by telling the reader what the run will find, namely how much of the second spend each unit receives. Keep it to one short line.

### Step 2. Collect the inputs

Ask for the base spend, then the multiple, then the number of units, one at a time. Read the base spend and the multiple as decimals, since money and a multiple may not be whole numbers. Read the number of units as a whole number, since you cannot have part of an office or region.

### Step 3. Scale the base spend

Multiply the base spend by the multiple. The result is the total for the second line.

### Step 4. Split evenly

Divide the second line total by the number of units. The result is the share that each unit receives.

### Step 5. Format the result

If the share comes out even with nothing left over, show it as a whole number. Otherwise keep the decimals. Do not round away digits the answer needs, because a value in millions can carry meaning in the third decimal. For example 0.744 million should not be cut back to 0.74.

### Step 6. Report the result

Print the share for each unit with short text that fits the scenario, such as the second spend each office receives. Do not print only the number.

---

## Example Trigger Prompts

- "Social spend is three times search spend. Split it across our 5 offices and tell me the social budget per office."
- "Set the paid line at 2.5 times the organic line, then divide the paid line evenly across 8 regions."
- "Each store gets an equal cut of the promo budget, and promo is four times the base budget. How much promo per store?"
- "I want one budget line sized as a multiple of another, then shared evenly across teams."
- "Scale our second cost line against the first and give me the per location amount."
