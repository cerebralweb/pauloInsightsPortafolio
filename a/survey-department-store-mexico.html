A SURVEY ON CENTENNIALS AND ALPHAS WANTS FOR A DEPARTMENT STORE IN MEXICO

En este caso ❧ For one of Mexico’s largest department store brands, I conducted a survey-driven study to understand the evolving expectations of Millennial, Centennial, and Alpha shoppers. r.

CLIENT

Department Store (one of the biggest brands in Mexico)

CLIENT CHALLENGES

The brand, a major department store in Mexico, invests heavily each season in sourcing inventory from China. While traditionally catering to affluent consumer segments (NSE A/B and C+ per AMAI standards), brand managers observed a growing influence of younger family members—Centennials and Alphas—within purchase decisions at the point of sale. Using BI-driven survey analysis, I helped assess how well current product categories aligned with the evolving behaviors and expectations of these emerging generations, providing strategic insights to guide future purchasing and inventory optimization.

    OBJECTIVES

  • Knowledge of young generations purchase expectations (Millenials, Centenials and Alpha)
  • Inventory and marketing alignment
  • Improve the next purchasing cycle of the brand in China
  • Optimize Inventory
  • Improve metrics in the e-commerce channel

Segments mentioned in this portfolio case

men and women. Alpha Gen: born 2010-2024, Zoomers: 1997-2012, Millenials: 1981-1996

SOLUTIONS

The analysis focused on assessing purchase habits and key consumer attributes, including shopping frequency, preferred product categories, and average spending levels across generational cohorts. Primary data from our custom survey was benchmarked against the client’s internal NielsenIQ datasets to validate trends, uncover gaps, and enhance the reliability of generational insights. This comparative approach enabled precise calibration between in-store observations and broader market intelligence.

METHODOLOGICAL CHALLENGES

  • We operationalized generational theory by defining the target universe through empirical variables: age (Centennials: 13–27; Alphas: 6–12), sex, and socioeconomic status (NSE C+ and above, per AMAI classification).
  • We applied a quota sampling method to ensure balanced representation across age groups, sex, and NSE strata within Mexico City. This approach enabled targeted insights while maintaining alignment with the brand’s primary consumer base.

    RESULTS & BUSINESS OUTCOMES

    Our analysis revealed significant generational differences in purchase behavior, with Centennials and Alphas demonstrating distinct preferences across product categories. Key findings included:

  • Key Insight: Significant variability was identified across generations and lifestyle segments—driven by the intersection of income level and consumption habits.
  • Centennials and Alphas exhibited a strong preference for digital channels, with over 70% indicating they would prefer to shop online for most categories.
  • Apparel and footwear emerged as the most frequently purchased categories, with over 60% of respondents indicating these as their top choices.
  • Beauty and home goods ranked highly among Alphas, while Centennials showed a strong interest in electronics and tech products.
  • Average spending levels varied significantly, with Centennials typically spending 20-30% more than Alphas across most categories.
  • In-store shopping remained important, with over 50% of respondents indicating they preferred to physically inspect products before purchase, particularly in categories like apparel and electronics.
  • Alphas showed a strong preference for experiential shopping, with 70% indicating they enjoyed interactive displays and in-store events, while Centennials preferred a more streamlined, efficient shopping experience.
  • Both generations expressed a desire for personalized shopping experiences, with 75% indicating they would appreciate tailored recommendations based on their previous purchases.
  • Environmental and social responsibility were important factors for both generations, with over 60% of respondents indicating they would prefer to shop with brands that demonstrate a commitment to sustainability and ethical sourcing.
  • Strategic Alignment: The brand adapted its category acquisition strategy to anticipate generational trends, particularly among Centennials and Alphas.
  • Acquisition of new categories and anticipation of emerging trends
  • The main new adquisition was an exclusive line of Nike products taylored for Alphas (focused on recycled materials, low cost and futuristic design.)
  • Product Category Expansion: The brand expanded its product offerings to include categories that resonated with younger generations, such as sustainable fashion, tech gadgets, and experiential products.
  • Product Category Focus: The brand prioritized categories that resonated with younger generations, such as apparel, footwear, and electronics.
  • Inventory Optimization: The brand adjusted its inventory levels to align with the preferences of these younger generations, ensuring that popular categories were well-stocked.
  • Performance Uplift: The initiative contributed to a 7% increase in e-commerce conversion and a 0.5% uplift in average ticket size, validating the business value of insight-led merchandising and messaging.
  • Sales Growth: The brand experienced a 10% increase in sales across the targeted categories within six months of implementing the recommendations, driven by improved alignment with consumer expectations.
  • Customer Satisfaction: Post-implementation surveys indicated a 15% increase in customer satisfaction scores among Centennials and Alphas, reflecting the positive impact of tailored product offerings and enhanced shopping experiences.
  • NPS: The brand saw a 20% increase in repeat purchases from Centennials and Alphas, indicating stronger brand loyalty and engagement.
  • Brand loyalty was notably higher among Centennials, with 65% indicating they would return to the same store for future purchases, compared to 45% of Alphas.
