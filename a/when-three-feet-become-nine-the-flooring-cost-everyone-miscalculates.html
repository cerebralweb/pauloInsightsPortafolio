WHEN THREE FEET BECOME NINE: THE FLOORING COST EVERYONE MISCALCULATES

In this article ❧ Procurement quotes often arrive in one unit while the space they apply to is measured in another. The risk is not the multiplication at the end but the conversion in the middle, where a length ratio quietly governs an area ratio. This problem shows how an area price must square its conversion factor before it scales to a room, and how Python turns that rule into a tool that never forgets the step.

The Problem

A data center operator wants to install raised floor panels in a new server room. The vendor quotes 45,000 for one square yard of paneling. The operator needs the cost based on the room layout, which is measured in square feet. One yard equals 3 feet, and the server room area is 120 square feet.

How much will the floor paneling cost?

  • a) 180,000
  • b) 6,000,000
  • c) 600,000
  • d) 1,800,000
Click to Reveal the Answer

Correct Answer: c) 600,000 ✅

Math Solution

One square yard equals 3 feet times 3 feet, which is 9 square feet. Dividing the 45,000 price by 9 gives 5,000 per square foot. Multiplying 5,000 by the 120 square foot room gives 600,000.

The linear ratio squares. A factor of 3 between lengths becomes a factor of 9 between areas.

Step-by-Step Breakdown

The following sequence shows how the final amount is derived:

  1. Convert one square yard: 3 ft × 3 ft = 9 square feet
  2. Price per square foot: 45,000 / 9 = 5,000
  3. Total for the room: 5,000 × 120 = 600,000

Historical Background

Measuring area for a price is one of the oldest practical uses of mathematics. In ancient Egypt, surveyors known as rope stretchers re-laid field boundaries each year after the Nile flood erased them, and the value of land followed directly from the area they recorded. The Rhind Mathematical Papyrus, copied around 1550 BCE, preserves worked methods for the area of rectangular and triangular plots, written for scribes who had to settle taxes and contracts.

The squaring trap in this problem is just as old. Whenever a society priced cloth, tiling, or land by one unit and measured the parcel in another, someone had to remember that doubling a side does not double the surface. The rule that a length ratio becomes its square in area has governed honest measurement, and honest billing, since the first surveyed field.

Python Version

The script converts any price per square yard into a total cost for a room measured in square feet, so the same logic works for carpet, tile, or paneling.

# What this does: works out the total cost of floor paneling for a room.
# Where it came from: a data center operator priced paneling per square yard but measured the room in square feet.
# The math idea: change the price to a per square foot rate, then multiply by the area.
# You type in: the price per square yard, how many feet are in a yard, and the room area in square feet.
# It prints: the total cost to panel the room.

# This function does the math.
def paneling_cost(price_per_sq_yard, feet_per_yard, area_sq_feet):
    # One square yard holds this many square feet. The side count is multiplied by itself.
    sq_feet_per_sq_yard = feet_per_yard * feet_per_yard
    # Find the price for one square foot.
    price_per_sq_foot = price_per_sq_yard / sq_feet_per_sq_yard
    # Multiply that price by the whole room area.
    return price_per_sq_foot * area_sq_feet

# Tell the reader what we will figure out.
print("Let's find the total cost to panel the server room.")

# Ask for the price of one square yard.
price_per_sq_yard = float(input("Price per square yard: "))

# Ask how many feet are in one yard.
feet_per_yard = float(input("Feet per yard: "))

# Ask for the room area in square feet.
area_sq_feet = float(input("Room area in square feet: "))

# Do the math.
result = paneling_cost(price_per_sq_yard, feet_per_yard, area_sq_feet)

# Show as a whole number when there is no leftover.
result = int(result) if result.is_integer() else result

# Print the answer with words that fit the scenario.
print("Total floor paneling cost for the server room:", result)

Run it in a terminal, type the three numbers, and it returns 600,000 for this room.

# Area Price Converter

This skill takes a price quoted per one unit of area, a linear conversion factor between two length units, and a room area in the smaller unit. It returns the total cost to cover that room. The key step is squaring the linear factor before converting the price, since area scales with the square of the linear ratio.

---

## Dependencies

None. Plain arithmetic only.

---

## Inputs

- **Price per square yard**: the quoted cost for one square unit, in money.
- **Feet per yard**: the linear conversion factor between the two length units, e.g. 3.
- **Room area in square feet**: the size of the space to cover, in the smaller unit.

---

## Steps

### Step 1 — Collect the three values

Ask for the price per square yard, the number of feet in one yard, and the room area in square feet. Treat all three as numbers that may have decimals.

### Step 2 — Square the linear factor

Multiply the feet per yard by itself to find how many square feet fit in one square yard. With 3 feet per yard, one square yard holds 9 square feet. Do not skip the squaring. Using the linear factor alone gives a cost wrong by that factor.

### Step 3 — Find the price per square foot

Divide the price per square yard by the squared factor from Step 2. This gives the cost of one square foot.

### Step 4 — Apply to the room

Multiply the price per square foot by the room area in square feet to get the total cost.

### Step 5 — Report the result

State the total cost in plain language tied to the scenario. Show it as a whole number when there is no leftover. Example output. Total floor cost for the room. 600,000.
