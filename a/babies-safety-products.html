It was the first time that I got in Pepe Ganga thinking like a father. I shamelessly affirm that in the past I used to take advantage of my walks through the shopping center to enter to that place just to see toys; me weakness were always the transformers. Now the reason to enter was Bernardo, and I was heading directly to the newborns section. By looking at the labels and details of each product, I began to think as an ethnographer - and marketer, and then a vision materialized: many visits, statistics, interviews, and this article

On one of those visits I scribbled the following list of topics to cover in my field journal:

The problem

How to sell a generation of parents concerned about safety? (In this text we analyze possible gaps in baby brands.) For that purpose we follow a hybrid method.

Steps followed in the investigation

After a couple of dizzying visits as an incognito buyer, I decided to interview some people. Finally I chose a woman, I explained my profession and the benefits of agreeing to be interviewed.

  • We build a case following qualitative techniques. We develop two interviews Ana , a 36-year-old woman, married, heterosexual, mother of a son, by profession an Engineer of software, with a master's degree in neural networks. Ana lives in Bogotá in a stratum 4 apartment (she is still paying for it), and is a contractor of a public company (complements its income as an hourly professor at a capital university). The first interview was structured (for the panel integration process), then two interviews were carried out, in two-hour sessions
  • Second, we investigate international safety standards for baby products.
  • Then we analyze statistics to establish the economic and demographic of this market.
  • Finally, we propose an anthropological investigation about the baby concepts that are used in our societies; we try to understand where do those concepts come from and how are they used today.

Get a true story: ("Ana")

Ana always wanted to be a mother: she almost did not succeed. The obstacles were not medical, nor sentimental; personally, I think the stress and movements that her professional career forced her prolonged the wait more than wanted. When reading the transcripts of our interviews, it seems to me that Ana is a typical case : in her experience we discovered that complex processes - apparently disconnected from motherhood - have an impact on decisions of consumption .

Insight

Bium: the brand is more for less . The Bium pens in the segment are very competitive because They are outstanding in two aspects: price and safety (key element in the value proposition in the segment of baby products).

Family

Ana was the first daughter of a more or less traditional marriage. Her mother did college, but did not complete them; her father, on the other hand, perfectly served as provider man and leader of the. House, thanks to her practice as a chemical engineer.

As the oldest of three siblings Ana felt inclined to be a meticulous student and to follow the steps of her father . A software engineer created computer programs and had to master ("later I discovered that the one I was looking to please was myself, not my dad", she concluded in one of the sections of the interview).

It is not a secret that in Latin America engineering has always been considered a trade for men . This brings me to my second consideration.

Gender

"I didn't want to be like my mom". When I asked Ana about the meaning of this phrase, she hesitated, and on her face she showed a grimace of shame. I insisted. I asked about the truncated profession of her mother (Law), about the age to she abandoned (19), what the causes were (the marriage with her father), and the consequences. Silence.

One last question occurred to me. Are you mad at your mother? --I said. I dont know. I ventured a Hypothesis: Maybe that had to do with your vocational choice - I suggested, but I got no answer.

It seemed clear to me that Ana's consumption decisions had profoundly influenced her familiar history ( Some psychologists refer in this way to family conflicts that inevitably we all drag.)

The key here is how to interpret the archetype behind Ana's decisions. Briefly: being an engineer was her way of handling various family ills , including the drama usually suffered by older children (fill father's shoes) and looks like mother (daughters' case) without being too much alike.

What decisions did Ana make in this context? Fundamentally one: putting off motherhood to focus completely in her professional career. She did a postgraduate degree in Spain (in cutting-edge technology - neural networks - which makes computers smarter , she married another engineer of software, and tried to get a job at a multinational company.

In other words, Ana decided to make a safe life route . All Ana's decisions are marked by this search for the safe (for example, it was safe to look like their father and insecure become a housewife, like her mother)

The need for insurance also affects consumption decisions. And with this information that we can analyze the relationship of an Ana as mom with the purchase of baby accessories.

Behavioral interpretation

The baby aftermarket is juicier than ever. Birth rates in almost all Latin American countries are rising. People like Ana want to buy. But I know It deals with people extremely concerned about security. Ana - the case with we accompany this research - have the ability to quickly know and understand the baby safety information (for example, from American standards ).

In addition to the diet of information and concern for safety, let's remember the weight of certain archetypes of family novels (which would not be unusual to see repeated over and over again in thousands of consumers.)

A Behavioral interpretation of "safe" products

Four factors (birth rate, higher educational levels, ability to access and interpret the information and family archetypes) allow to understand Ana's consumption decision in the moment of buying a playpen for your baby: do not choose the most expensive, nor the largest, nor the most decorated, nor the one have more accessories: choose the safest according to international safety standards

It is not a lack of budget : it is a complex security concern.
