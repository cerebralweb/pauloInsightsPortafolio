ADD BEFORE YOU CONVERT: UNIT MATH FOR BIOBANK SAMPLE INTAKE

In this article ❧ Regulated facilities — biobanks, clinical labs, pharmaceutical warehouses — record weight in specific units for compliance and chain-of-custody purposes. When individual consignments arrive documented in ounces but intake forms require pounds, staff must aggregate and convert correctly before a single entry can be made. This problem demonstrates how unit conversion combined with mixed-number arithmetic underpins a routine but high-stakes data-entry task, and how a small Python tool can eliminate manual error entirely.

A lab coordinator is consolidating two fragile consignments of cryopreserved tissue samples before transferring them to a biobank facility. One consignment weighs 8 ounces and the other weighs 11 ounces. Given that one pound equals 16 ounces — or equivalently, that 1 ounce = 1/16 pound — what is the combined weight of the two consignments in pounds, as required on the biobank intake form?

  • a) 1 and 3/16
  • b) 1 and 16/3
  • c) 1
  • d) 16
Click to Reveal the Answer

Correct Answer: a) 1 and 3/16 pounds ✅

Math Structure Preserved

The problem requires two sequential operations: addition of the two ounce values, followed by unit conversion from ounces to pounds using the equivalence 1 oz = 1/16 lb. Because 19 × (1/16) = 19/16, and 19/16 is not a whole number, the result is expressed as a mixed number — a whole part plus a proper fraction.

Key insight: the remainder after dividing 19 by 16 becomes the numerator of the fractional part, while 16 remains the denominator — giving exactly 1 and 3/16.

Step-by-Step Breakdown

The following sequence shows how the final amount is derived:

  1. Unit equivalence: 1 oz = 1/16 lb, so every ounce is one sixteenth of a pound
  2. Total weight in ounces: 8 + 11 = 19 oz
  3. Convert to pounds: 19 × (1/16) = 19/16 lb
  4. Express as a mixed number: 19 ÷ 16 = 1 remainder 3 → 1 and 3/16 lb
  5. Conclusion: The combined consignment weighs 1 and 3/16 pounds — the figure the coordinator enters on the biobank intake form.

Why This Math Matters for the Business Problem

Cryopreserved tissue samples are among the most weight-sensitive materials handled in clinical and research logistics. Biobank intake systems record weight in pounds for regulatory compliance and chain-of-custody documentation. A coordinator who adds the two consignment weights incorrectly — or applies the ounces-to-pounds conversion in the wrong direction — risks intake rejection, sample misidentification, or audit failures. Even a fractional error on a biobank form can trigger a quality hold on irreplaceable biological material.

Mathematical Background

This problem applies unit conversion combined with mixed-number arithmetic. Because 1 ounce equals exactly 1/16 of a pound, converting any ounce measurement to pounds is equivalent to multiplying by the fraction 1/16. When the result is not a whole number, it is expressed as a mixed number: a whole part plus a proper fraction. One conceptual note worth remembering is that the remainder in a division problem becomes the numerator of the fractional part, while the divisor becomes the denominator — a relationship that trips up many people when converting units under time pressure.

Python Version

The script below generalises the calculation so any two ounce-based consignment weights and any conversion rate can be entered at runtime, returning the combined weight as a properly formatted mixed number.

def combined_weight_in_pounds(consignment_1_oz, consignment_2_oz, oz_per_pound):
    total_oz = consignment_1_oz + consignment_2_oz
    whole_pounds = total_oz // oz_per_pound
    remainder_oz = total_oz % oz_per_pound
    return whole_pounds, remainder_oz, oz_per_pound

print("Biobank Intake — Combined Consignment Weight Calculator")
consignment_1_oz = float(input("Enter weight of first consignment (oz): "))
consignment_2_oz = float(input("Enter weight of second consignment (oz): "))
oz_per_pound      = float(input("Enter ounces per pound (standard: 16): "))

whole, remainder, denom = combined_weight_in_pounds(consignment_1_oz, consignment_2_oz, oz_per_pound)

if remainder == 0:
    print(f"Combined consignment weight for biobank intake form: {int(whole)} lb")
else:
    print(f"Combined consignment weight for biobank intake form: {int(whole)} and {int(remainder)}/{int(denom)} lb")

Run the script in Windows Terminal by typing python, pasting the code, pressing Enter, and supplying the three values when prompted — the program returns the combined weight formatted as a mixed number ready for the intake form.

# Biobank Intake — Combined Consignment Weight Converter

This skill takes the individual weights of two consignments (in ounces) and a
pound-to-ounce conversion rate, combines them, and expresses the total weight in
pounds as a mixed number (whole pounds plus a remaining fraction). It is designed
for intake coordinators, lab staff, or logistics analysts who need to record combined
weight on a regulated facility form where pounds are the required unit.

---

## Dependencies

## Inputs

- **First consignment weight**: A number in ounces. May be a whole number or decimal.
- **Second consignment weight**: A number in ounces. May be a whole number or decimal.
- **Conversion rate**: The number of ounces that equal one pound. Standard value is 16.

---

## Steps

### Step 1 — Greet and orient the user

Print a short title line identifying the tool as a biobank intake weight calculator,
so the user knows what the program does before entering any numbers.

### Step 2 — Collect the three input values

Prompt the user to enter each of the three inputs one at a time: the weight of the
first consignment in ounces, the weight of the second consignment in ounces, and
the ounces-per-pound conversion rate. Accept decimal values for all three.

### Step 3 — Compute the total weight in ounces

Add the two consignment weights together to obtain the combined total in ounces.

### Step 4 — Convert to pounds using the unit equivalence

Divide the total ounces by the conversion rate. Separate the result into two parts:
the whole number of pounds (using integer division) and the leftover ounces that did
not complete a full pound (using the remainder operation).

### Step 5 — Format the result as a mixed number

If there is no remainder, display the result as a whole number of pounds. If there
is a remainder, display the result as whole pounds plus a fraction, where the
remainder is the numerator and the conversion rate is the denominator.

### Step 6 — Print the final result with business context

Output the combined weight with a short label that connects the number to its
purpose — identifying it as the figure to enter on the biobank intake form.
Do not print the number alone.
